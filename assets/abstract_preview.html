Virus- and interferon alpha-induced transcriptomes of the "microbat" Myotis daubentonii (suborder Yangochiroptera)

Question


Interferons (IFN-α/β) are held responsible for the high tolerance of bats to zoonotic viruses [1,2]. However, earlier studies focused mainly on the IFN system of megabats (suborder Yinpterochiroptera) [3]. Here, we present transcriptomes of cells from the "microbat" Myotis daubentonii (suborder Yangochiroptera, family Vespertilionidae) responding at 6h and 24h to either an IFN-inducing virus or treatment with IFN.


Methods


We stimulated M. daubentonii cells either with a strong IFN inducer virus or with type I IFN. Moreover, we measured global cellular transcription at two different time points and from three biological replicates using Illumina short-read RNA-Seq. Since no reference genome for M. daubentonii is publicly available so far, we used the genome of the closely related "microbat" M. lucifugus as a reference for RNA quantification, normalization, and differential expression analysis. Additionally, we de novo assembled the M. daubentonii transcriptomes using different assembly tools and a clustering approach. Finally, we verified uniquely virus-regulated genes by RT-qPCR.


Results


Our data reveal novel genes that are only triggered by the virus, either in both humans and Myotis (CCL4, IFNL3, CH25H), or exclusively in Myotis (STEAP4). Moreover, Myotis expresses a series of conserved IFN-stimulated genes (ISGs) and an unusually high number of paralogs of the antiviral ISG BST2 (tetherin), but lacks several megabat ISGs (EMC2, FILIP1, IL17RC, OTOGL, SLC24A1).


Conclusions


In contrast to megabats, there is neither a genomic expansion nor an unusually high baseline expression of IFNs. Thus, Yangochiroptera possess an IFN system with some specific features, but no major qualitative or quantitative differences to other mammals.
